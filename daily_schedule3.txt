Feeling tired can be influenced by several factors, including sleep quality, exercise, diet, and lifestyle habits. Here are some suggestions to address your concerns:

### Sleep
1. **Consistency:** Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends. This helps regulate your body's internal clock.
2. **Quality:** Ensure your sleep environment is conducive to rest. This means a comfortable mattress, cool room temperature, and minimal noise and light.
3. **Duration:** Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night. Track your sleep for a week to find your optimal sleep duration.

### Exercise
1. **Frequency:** Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity each week, plus muscle-strengthening activities on two or more days a week.
2. **Type:** Incorporate a mix of cardiovascular exercises (like cycling, running, or dancing) and strength training (like bodyweight exercises or lifting weights).
3. **Consistency:** Regular exercise improves energy levels and overall well-being. Start with small, achievable goals and gradually increase the intensity and duration.

### Additional Tips
1. **Diet:** Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Avoid excessive sugar and caffeine, especially before bed.
2. **Hydration:** Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated.
3. **Stress Management:** Practice stress-reducing activities like meditation, deep breathing, or hobbies you enjoy.
4. **Limit Screen Time:** Reduce screen time, especially before bed. Blue light from screens can interfere with your sleep quality.

### Sample Routine to Increase Energy
1. **Morning:**
   - Wake up at the same time each day.
   - Engage in a short exercise routine.
   - Eat a nutritious breakfast.
   - Practice a mindfulness activity.
2. **Day:**
   - Take short breaks during work to move around.
   - Stay hydrated and eat balanced meals.
3. **Evening:**
   - Engage in relaxing activities to unwind.
   - Limit screen time before bed.
   - Establish a bedtime routine to signal your body it's time to sleep.
